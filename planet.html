K°epuhthenoh and Auh/hl/ihth

Ok°s/hl/ihd

Size and Weight: 3.5ft body, 8ft wingspan/ 11lbs
Lifespan: 20 years
Habitat: The sky and waters
Diet: Bugs and fish
Distant Ancestors: Girrafe-life creature? way different limbs,,,

Ok°s/hl/id are used by so Enausq'/hl/ to send messages to far off villages. They draw them to dry areas using their hands to splash into buckets of water, imitating the sound of the sea. They hold fish out for them, and when they're close enough, slap various paint colors on parts of their body as code for different things.

They typically travel in groups of 1000-4000 and move as a sort of hivemind. It is highly advised to not attack an Ok°s/hl/ihd. They are blind and weak, but in their large numbers their sharp beaks can do mass amounts of damage.

  • They spend a majority of their lives in the air and rarely land.
  • Their path through the land never changes, and they rarely move away from the exact trail, only changing occasionally or with the seasons so prey don't become sparse.
  • They tuck their necks into their bodies during flight to increase their aerodynamics and keep their neck muscles from straining.
  • They don't have eyes, but the black areas on their head work as sensors to determine night and day by sensing the suns light and heat.
  • The sacks on their faces work to sense the environment around them using echolocation from the high pitched chirps they constantly let out. This allows them to stay in the same ordered group and on the same path.


  • They are a yellowish-grey in color to camoflauge into the color of the sky when viewed at from below by fish

Smau/hl/ Au/hl/etheoh/hl/ (Water-Eaters)

Size and Weight: 4ft / 120lbs
Lifespan: 45 years
Habitat: Coastlines and tropical wetlands
Diet: Microorganisms, algae, plankton, small smails
Distant Ancestors: ---

Water-Eaters are known for their style of feeding, which is to filter through water for bacteria and microorganisms using the same fibrous mouth as a Baleen whale.

  • They use their two front appendages to scrape algae from rocks and stir up still water to feed more effectively. The two tusks work to effectively push away larger objects in the water.
  • They have brightly colored back ends filled with poison in order to deter predators attacking while they feed.

Ooheihaudeih

Size and Weight: 8ft w/o horns, 900lbs
Lifespan: 13 years
Habitat: A majority of Kepuh and Auhlith, usually in denser forested areas. Sparse in Sohqauthuh.
Diet: ---
Distant Ancestors: Enausq'/hl/

Built like a bison and as common as deer, many folklore tales revolve around Ooheihaudeih due to their hands and uncanny resemblance to an Enausq'/hl/ face. In the dark, it is an unwelcome sight.

  • Killing them is slightly looked down on due to the resemblance, but their fur is the best source of hair extentions.
  • They are herded by some, but because they are still dangerous, it is not extremely common.
  • If you do kill one, their horns make wonderful weapons in combat, although horned combat is difficult to learn, and doing the needed alterations to the horns is not a common skill.

  • The front of their faces are covered in a hard plate, where their noses lies attached at the bottom. They can drink water through their nose, but can't move this plate, so they have a long tongue to pick up food.
  • They have four eyes, two to the sides and two at the front

Vegetation

K°epuhthenoh

Glow Moss

Glow Moss is a thin and brittle plant that grows on rocks and trees, most often on the dark side of mountains and at the entrance of caves. It glows faintly when crushed, and is often collected by Einasvel and hung on the branches of their homes to be used to find the way at night.

When the sun sets, they go around and crush all of the bundles.

The color is based on the nutrient levels and content of where it grew, and ranges wildly. Some examples are (from most to least common):

  • Dusty blue grows in dry locations with lots of direct sunlight.
  • Lilac in locations with more indirect than direct light. It grows the longest out of any other variation at around a foot.
  • Green
  • Yellow
  • Pale red-orange
  • Dark green. Glows the dimmest. Commonly used to create hairstyles. in places with low direct light and heavy water.
  • Deep red grows in wetter areas with low indirect light, such as the sides of large rock formations and caves. It's a common meal for creatures in the area who don't have much else to eat, so it grows short and dark to blend into the environment. It is hard to remove and is much softer than other variants.
